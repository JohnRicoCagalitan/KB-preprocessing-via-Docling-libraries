## I. Overview

The  grading  system  of  Xavier  University  -  Ateneo  de  Cagayan  is  used  to  evaluate  the  academic performance  of  students  in  each  course.  Grades  are  assigned  by  instructors  based  on  the  student's achievement of course learning outcomes and completion of course requirements.

Grades are recorded at the end of the academic term and form part of the student's official academic record maintained by the university registrar.

## II. Letter Grade Scale

Xavier  University  -  Ateneo  de  Cagayan  uses  a  letter-based  grading  system  with  corresponding percentage ranges.

| Letter Grade   | Percentage Range   |

| B-             | 68.00 - 75.99      |
| C              | 60.00 - 67.99      |
| D              | 50.00 - 59.99      |
| F              | 49.99 below        |

## III. Special Grade Marks

Certain remarks may appear instead of letter grades in specific situations.

## INC - Incomplete

An Incomplete (INC) grade may be given when a student fails to complete some course requirements due to valid reasons.

## AF - Failure due to Absences

An AF (Failure due to Absences) is a grade remark assigned to a student who exceeds the maximum number of allowable absences in a course. When a student accumulates absences beyond the limit set by  university policy, the instructor may  record  an  AF  regardless  of  the  student's  academic performance in other course requirements.

Receiving  an  AF  indicates  that  the  student  did  not  meet  the  attendance  requirements  necessary  to complete  the  course  successfully.  This  remark  is  treated  as  a  failing  mark  for  the  course  and  may affect the student's academic standing and grade point average.

Attendance policies,  including the maximum number of allowable absences, are typically defined in the university's academic regulations and are communicated to students at the beginning of the course through the syllabus.

## W - Withdrawn

A  W  remark  indicates  that  a  student  officially  withdrew  from  the  course  before  the  deadline  for withdrawal. This remark does not affect the student's grade point average.

## IV. Computation of Final Grades

The computation of final grades in courses at Xavier University - Ateneo de Cagayan is typically based on a weighted evaluation of academic requirements such as examinations, quizzes, participation, and course outputs.

The specific weight distribution of these components is defined by the course instructor and is usually communicated to students through the course syllabus at the beginning of the semester.

In some courses, grading may follow a structured weight distribution similar to the following example used in university course management systems:

| Component                | Weight   |
|--------------------------|----------|
| Prelim Exam              | 7.00%    |
| Midterm Exam             | 10.50%   |
| Engagement/Participation | 10.50%   |
| Semi-Final Exam          | 7.00%    |
| Final Exam               | 30.00%   |

| Quizzes   | 35.00%   |
|-----------|----------|

The total of all grading components equals 100% of the final course grade. The exact components and their corresponding weights may vary depending on the course and instructor.

## V. Quality Point Index (QPI)

The  Quality  Point  Index  (QPI)  is  the  numerical  measure  used  by  Xavier  University  -  Ateneo  de Cagayan to evaluate a student's overall academic performance. The QPI is similar to the Grade Point Average (GPA) used in other universities.

It  represents  the  weighted  average  of  a  student's  grades  across  all  completed  courses,  taking  into account the number of units assigned to each course.

## VI. Academic Records

Final  grades  are  recorded  and  maintained  by  the  university registrar as part of the student's official academic record. These records are used for:

- Academic evaluation
- Scholarship eligibility
- Graduation requirements
- Transcript of records

The registrar ensures compliance with university policies and national higher education regulations.
